I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at North Carolina State University, where I am advised by [Dr. Aritra Mitra](https://amitra2.wordpress.ncsu.edu/). My research lies at the intersection of reinforcement learning, statistical learning, robust statistics, and control theory.

Broadly, I am interested in developing learning algorithms that are reliable in imperfect real-world environments, where data may be corrupted, heavy-tailed, asynchronous, adversarially generated, or collected by unreliable agents. A central theme of my work is to understand how sequential decision-making algorithms behave when the feedback used for learning cannot be fully trusted.

Much of my current research focuses on robust reinforcement learning, with an emphasis on finite-sample guarantees for \(Q\)-Learning and temporal-difference learning under adversarial corruption. I am particularly interested in asynchronous and federated reinforcement learning settings, where multiple agents collect data from a common environment and communicate information to a central learner under limited communication and possible adversarial behavior.

Before joining NC State, I completed my M.Tech. in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, and my B.E. in Electrical Engineering at Jadavpur University.

My long-term goal is to develop reinforcement learning and control algorithms that are both statistically reliable and operationally safe, even when deployed in environments with corrupted feedback, distributional uncertainty, and unreliable data sources.
